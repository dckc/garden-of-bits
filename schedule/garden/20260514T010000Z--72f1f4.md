# Hourly scholar library cycle

A periodical scholar cycle that fires every UTC hour at :00. Dispatched by the [timekeeper](../../../../../roles/timekeeper/AGENT.md) into a [scholar](../../../../../roles/scholar/AGENT.md) subagent in a fresh dispatch root. The scholar follows its standard per-cycle procedure: sync the journal, drain the `to: scholar` inbox, process up to its section budget (about 3 to 5 source documents or 25 section writes), update `journal/library/` indexes, journal a `result` entry, exit.

## Why hourly

The supported recurrence shapes (per `<garden-root>/skills/scheduling/SKILL.md` § Recurrence rule shape) include `hourly-at-<MM>-<timezone>`, `daily-...`, `weekly-...`, and `one-shot`. The scholar's cadence (`<garden-root>/roles/scholar/AGENT.md` § Cadence) wants 1800-3600s in idle mode and ≤1800s in active mode. The supported shapes do not offer sub-hour recurrence, so the closest match is hourly. When the inbox is empty the cycle drains quickly and exits; when there is backlog the cycle processes its budget and defers the rest to the next fire. This matches the cadence skill's intent without sub-hour resolution.

If the inbox accumulates faster than hourly can drain, the maintainer can register a second event at `hourly-at-30-UTC` (or extend the recurrence-rule grammar with sub-hour shapes, a gardener change).

## Current state at registration

12 `to: scholar` `ingest-source` messages are pending in the inbox at this event's creation: 2 re-queued from the first scholar cycle (`docs/get-started.md`, `packages/ses/README.md`) plus 10 fresh asks for the remaining `docs/` files and several flagship package READMEs (`eventual-send`, `marshal`, `pass-style`, `patterns`, `exo`, `daemon`). At the scholar's per-cycle budget, the first 3 to 4 fires should drain most of the queue; thereafter the loop idles until new asks arrive.

## Indefinite-loop intent

The event has no end date. It fires every hour until the maintainer removes it (move the latest pending file to `_fired/` and don't roll over, or set its `recurrence:` to `one-shot`). The timekeeper handles recurrence rollover after each fire (per `<garden-root>/skills/scheduling/SKILL.md` § Firing an event step 7): on each fire it computes the next trigger via `next-trigger.sh hourly-at-00-UTC <current-trigger>` and writes a fresh event file before archiving the current one.
